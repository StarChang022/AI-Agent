## 1. SSL / Cloudflare

Using Cloudflare does not automatically mean your scorecard is free of SSL/TLS issues. SecurityScorecard can still flag findings in a few scenarios:

### CDN IP rotation: 

Cloudflare rotates IPs frequently, which can cause SecurityScorecard to observe your certificate from many IPs within a window of time, counting each observation as a unique finding. [TLS Weak Cipher Suite]

### Out-of-date certificates on specific CDN servers: 

Sometimes the CDN serves outdated certificates or cipher suites on specific servers, even if your main website appears fine. It's worth checking the specific IP flagged in the finding. [TLS Weak Cipher Suite]

### Weak protocols: 

If any endpoint behind Cloudflare still supports weak TLS protocols, SecurityScorecard will flag it. If the configuration genuinely cannot be changed due to business requirements, you can add a Public Issue Comment to provide context to anyone viewing your scorecard. [SSL/TLS Weak Protocol]

### Recommendation: 

Check the specific IP and port flagged in the SSL finding on your scorecard — don't assume Cloudflare covers everything automatically.

## 2. Old/Unused IP Address

If the IP no longer belongs to your organization, you can request its removal from your Digital Footprint:

1. Go to the IPs tab in your Digital Footprint.
2. Select the IP and click Manage.
3. Review the attribution evidence SecurityScorecard used to link it to you.
4. Select the category "IP should not be linked to this Scorecard" and provide an explanation (and any supporting evidence) demonstrating it's not your asset. [Manage Assets]

### Important timing note: 

Even after your removal request is approved, the IP will show a "Removed" status but may still display a score impact for 2–3 days while the score is recalculated and published. This delay is automatic and cannot be manually adjusted. [IP Removed but Score Still Impacted]

### Also note: 

if findings were observed on that IP while it was still attributed to you, those findings will decay according to SecurityScorecard's scoring methodology (e.g., malware findings age out after 30 days). If you have evidence the IP was not yours at the time of detection, you can also submit the finding for resolution through the Issue Resolution Process. [Misattribution]
