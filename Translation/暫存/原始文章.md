Great news — if you're no longer using that IP, there are clear steps you can take to address these findings. Here's how to handle each aspect:

### **Why Is the IP Still Appearing?**

SecurityScorecard's scoring process takes several days to complete. If the IP was recently rotated or released, it may still appear on your scorecard temporarily due to the pipeline delay (data is gathered → processed → displayed over ~2 days). Additionally, DNS attribution has a TTL of 1 day, meaning the IP can remain attributed for a short period after the DNS entry stops resolving to it. **These findings will drop automatically once the attribution TTL expires.** [[**Rotated IPs FAQ**](https://support.securityscorecard.com/hc/en-us/articles/37925395410203-Findings-still-shown-on-already-rotated-IPs)]

### **What You Should Do**

### **1. Manage the IP in Your Digital Footprint**

The most direct action is to dispute the IP attribution through your Digital Footprint:

1. Go to the **IPs** tab in your Digital Footprint.
2. Select the IP and click **Manage**.
3. Review the attribution evidence SecurityScorecard used to link the IP to your domain.
4. Select the category **"IP should not be linked to this Scorecard"** if you can demonstrate the IP is not correctly attributed to your domain.
5. Provide an explanation and any supporting evidence, then submit.

If approved, the IP and all associated findings will be removed from your Scorecard (within approximately 72 hours). [[**Manage Assets**](https://support.securityscorecard.com/hc/en-us/articles/45103604591899-Manage-assets-or-request-their-removal)]

### **2. For the SSL/TLS and Cipher Suite Findings**

If the IP is still showing findings before the attribution is resolved, you can also submit a **False Positive** resolution for the SSL/TLS and cipher suite findings:

- Select the finding → click **"Other resolutions"** → **"I cannot reproduce this issue and I think it's incorrect"**.
- A near-instant re-scan will be triggered. If the issue is no longer observed, it will be approved automatically. If it is still observed, a Support Case will be created for further review. [[**Remediation Validation**](https://support.securityscorecard.com/hc/en-us/articles/40395923414939-Remediation-validation-and-Support-ticket-creation)]

### **3. For the CVE/Vulnerability Findings**

For the High and Medium severity vulnerability findings, SecurityScorecard can remove them if you can demonstrate that:

- The IP was never used by your company (misattribution), or
- At the time of the finding, the IP did not yet belong to your organization.

You would need to submit a resolution request with supporting evidence. [[**Vulnerability Findings**](https://support.securityscorecard.com/hc/en-us/articles/115002965683-Vulnerability-in-Last-Observation)]

---

**In summary:** Start by managing the IP in your Digital Footprint and selecting "IP should not be linked to this Scorecard." This is the most efficient path, as it will address all associated findings at once if approved.
